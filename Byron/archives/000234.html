My notes on Our day in Trondheim

Trondheim. Folkehøgskole day, or something.
Aparently there were 'all' the Folkehøgskoles from the area attending this 'event'. I may have seen one other school. But there I go not starting at the begining.


We woke up about 9, and hopp off the boat into the center of town. I love that. The rest of the school is just unloading the cars as we walk up, somehow that worked out, and we start setting up camp. We have half a block along the Walking-Street to setup banners and booths and other assorted Lets Show off the School type stuff, including pancakes. Which is kinda odd, we give out pancakes to entice the public into talking with sombody and learning about the school. What was really different was the open fire we cooked them on. Camp fire. No stove, just a large metal disk with firewood on it, on fire. Kinda cool. And nice and warm in the below freezing weather.


The church! Nidarose, or something like that, is amazingly old and equally beatiful. We walked aound for a hour or so. It's kinda like London's Westminster Abbey, only with less dead people. This trip to the church highlighed something else. The school has something magical about it. It works. Which may not seem to impressiive, but given how little they plan this is truley remarkable. The 'plan' for this church visit was for

The Force and the school.
go see the cathedral.
Go sing. Leave.
Cruz the city
Burger King for 99 Kroner. Big Deal.
Comic books, movies closed.
Get back on boat. low tide.
putter out, beutiful
cruze back, philosphy.
Sleep well.
